My Story

Originally, I entered college as a Chinese Studies major but I soon double majored in International Relations with an emphasis in World Trade and Development and an area emphasis in East Asia. This stemmed from my interest in learning about people, cultures, history, and languages. My initial goal was to become a Chinese to English translator or interpreter to help bridge the language barrier many Chinese immigrants face in the United States.

During the pandemic, I worked at the Oakland Chinatown Chamber of Commerce as an Administrative Assistant and worked with a lot of Chinese immigrant business owners and community members who were having difficulty adapting to changes due to COVID-19. Businesses were closing because they could not adapt to the shift to online services such as offering delivery through food delivery apps. Community members were unable to attend community workshops because they did not know how to use Zoom. This was due to the lack of language options and high difficulty of some apps and websites. I knew that there had to be a way to improve these products so they could be more inclusive of those who suffer from language barriers or low digital literacy. While researching solutions to help members of my community, I learned I could be a part of that solution through user experience design and create products that are functional, inclusive, and cross cultural.
